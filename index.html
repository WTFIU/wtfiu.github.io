Welcome to the edge of logic.

wtfiu is a space for creative digital experiments — a small universe where code, art, and imagination collide. Every pixel here has a purpose, every animation is intentional, and every project is built to explore how technology can express creativity. This site evolves constantly, serving as a playground for web concepts, design experiments, and coding ideas that might not fit anywhere else.

The goal is simple: to build things that make you curious. From glowing gradients and smooth motion to interactive JavaScript utilities, wtfiu mixes fun with function. Some projects you’ll find here are purely visual — light trails, color pulses, and playful illusions. Others are tools that help automate or enhance small everyday tasks, made with a sense of experimentation rather than perfection.

This site is also a learning log — every experiment adds something new, whether it’s mastering CSS animations, exploring APIs, or testing how web performance changes under creative pressure. Each project here started with a single question: “What if…?” That question fuels everything on wtfiu.

Beyond aesthetics, there’s intent: building a place that encourages curiosity, creativity, and discovery. Visitors can expect occasional updates — sometimes a new idea, sometimes a full release, sometimes a hidden concept page. The site’s tone is minimal but alive, glowing with motion and depth while staying simple enough to navigate intuitively.

wtfiu is not about trends or clicks — it’s about exploration. It’s a reminder that the web can still be weird, beautiful, and experimental all at once. Every page here exists as an invitation to imagine and to build. Whether you’re here to get inspired, to learn, or to simply watch light move across a dark screen, you’re part of the creative process that keeps this world expanding.
